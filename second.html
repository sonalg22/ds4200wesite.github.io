More About My Passion

Through my dance and music experiences, I’ve learned that the arts are not just hobbies but a means of self-expression and connection with others. Dance, especially, has pushed me out of my comfort zone and allowed me to explore different aspects of myself. Indian Classical dance, with its precision and grace, has taught me discipline and storytelling, while hip-hop and fusion have encouraged me to let loose and experiment with new movements. The fusion of these styles excites me—there's something empowering about blending tradition with modernity and creating something new and unique every time I step onto the dance floor. My performances are not just about choreography, but about how the music and how the movements come together to tell a certain story and evoke emotion within the audience.
Music, on the other hand, has become a newfound passion that keeps me grounded. Whether I’m playing bass or piano, there’s something deeply personal and fulfilling about creating sound and being part of something bigger—whether it’s with my set on stage or if it’s a random jamming session with my friends. I love learning new instruments and performing for live audiences. It has challenged me in the best way possible, forcing me to adapt and grow. I’m definitely still learning and growing–I shy away from lead vocals because it’s such a vulnerable task to put all my emotion through my voice. It’s much easier for me through dance. Both dance and music allow me to continually evolve, and I can’t wait to see where these creative journeys take me next.

Things I love about Music and Dance:

  • The performance high.
  • A standing ovation.
  • The release of emotion when I'm by myself.
  • The friends I've made along my journey.
  • Lights, camera, loud music.
  • Floor vibrations from a bass.
  • Adrenaline rush on stage.
  • The burn in my thighs from practicing too much.
  • A new guitar tone.
  • Learning, more than teaching
  • Making a vision come true.
  • Envoking emotions in my audiences.
